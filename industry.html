The Evolution of the Brazilian Energy Matrix

The Transition to Diversity

Since the year 2000, the supply of energy in Brazil has evolved from being heavily dependent on hydropower to a diverse, dual-track supply. While hydropower, biofuels, and waste historically dominated, in the years 2023 and 2024, solar and other renewable energy sources rose significantly to the point where they surpassed the coal and nuclear energy supply.

Despite this renewable surge, the supply of oil and oil products has always been high. With the exception of a temporary drop in 2020, the supply increased steadily through 2024. Additionally, the production of energy from biofuels and waste sources has seen a marked increase since 2022, staying slightly above its supply requirements.

Domestic Consumption Profile

Within Brazil, the largest consumer of energy—specifically oil and gas—is the transportation sector, accounting for 37.3% of total consumption. This is closely followed by industry at 33.7%, the residential sector at 12%, with the remainder utilized for agriculture and forestry purposes.

One notable observation is the increasing reliance on oil and gas for electricity generation. In the early 2000s, Brazil was producing about 2.7 million TJ of crude oil, but this number has tripled over the last two decades to meet growing power demands.

From Consumer to Global Exporter

There is a significant trend in the production of domestic energy. According to the IEA, domestic production has increased to the point where Brazil now produces almost double the oil and oil products required for its own consumption. Having fully satisfied domestic demand, the country has firmly transitioned into a net exporter.

Since 2000, the trend in overall energy exports has increased by 561%, reaching 10.6% in 2024 and ranking Brazil first among South American countries for regional exports. Specifically, crude oil exports have skyrocketed. Since 2015, Brazil's share of exporting crude oil was 40.3%, representing an 8,791% trend increase. Domestic production of crude oil now accounts for 166% of the total energy supply production.

The Natural Gas Market

The share of natural gas in the total energy supply was 8.6% in 2024, with the overall amount of natural gas supply increasing 228% since 2000.

Of this total supply, 74% was produced domestically in Brazil—a segment that has seen a 217% domestic production increase over the last two decades. The remaining 25% of the natural gas supply was imported, with those imports increasing by 265% since the year 2000.

Strategic Infrastructure

The primary success of the industry stems from deep-water Pre-salt layer exploration. The Búzios field—capable of producing 1 million barrels per day—and the P-78 FPSO vessel are critical components of Brazil's infrastructure. These assets not only bolster oil production but also allow for massive gas exports to the mainland via interconnection with the ROTA 3 gas pipeline.
