Industry.

The Evolution of the Brazilian Energy Matrix

Industry Matrix:

Brazil has one of the world’s most balanced energy matrices in the world. While the global average for renewable sources is much lower, Brazil’s total energy supply is nearly 50% renewable. Fossil fuel is still used for industrial growth and transportation.

  • Oil and Oil products (35.6%): The largest part of the energy sector and it stays high regardless of price because it fuels the nation's transportation (trucking and shipping).
  • Biofuels and waste (33.4%): Brazil is a world leader in ethanol and biodiesel. This high percentage allows Brazil to be more stable during world shocks and unexpected problems.
  • Hydropower (11.4%): for electricity production.
  • Natural gas (8.6%): A growth area. While there is a low production of natural gas, the country is trying to increase production.

Trade Dynamics: Production, Imports and Exports

The data shows a contradicting reality for Brazil: Brazil is rich in oil and a major oil producer in South America, but vulnerable in the natural gas sector.

  • Crude Oil Surplus: Brazil produces significantly more oil than its internal needs. Domestic production is roughly 166% of the domestic consumption. This allows Brazil to export oil to China, Europe, US, and other countries.
  • Refined Products: While being a major exporter, it still imports specific refined products like diesel to meet the demand.
  • Natural Gas (The dependency): Brazil imports 30% of its natural gas.

Reserves and Long-Term Value:

Petrobras' significant "inventory" in the ground serves as a strategic "bank account" for future expansion and successfully secures the company's long-term value. Over 75% of Brazil's 16.8 billion barrels of proven (1P) oil reserves are found in the abundant deep-water Pre-salt strata. This concentration offers a clear competitive advantage; the reserves are large and incredibly productive, with individual pre-salt wells producing up to 50,000 barrels per day—a performance metric that greatly surpasses average well outputs in the Middle East or the United States. The company's natural gas portfolio, which has proved reserves of around 546 billion cubic meters, complements this oil richness and strengthens the basis for long-term energy production and financial stability.
